## **1. Aortic Dissection**

#### **1.1 Definition & Classification**

Acute aortic dissection is defined as a tear in the aortic intima allowing blood to enter between the layers of the aortic wall, creating a false lumen and leading to propagation of the dissection. ([NCBI\)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441963/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

The most common classification schema is the Stanford system:

- *Type A* involves the ascending aorta (proximal to the brachiocephalic branch) requires emergent surgical management. [\(NCBI](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441963/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- *Type B* involves the descending aorta (distal to the left subclavian artery) often managed medically if uncomplicated. [\(StatPearls\)](https://www.statpearls.com/point-of-care/164104?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **1.2 Why It Matters**

This is a life-threatening vascular emergency. Without treatment, the mortality is very high (≈ 50% within 48 h for ascending dissections). ([Medscape](https://emedicine.medscape.com/article/756835-overview?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

Rapid recognition, diagnosis, and appropriate intervention (surgical or medical) are essential for survival. [\(Ahajournals](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001106?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

#### **1.3 Clinical Presentation**

**Pain characteristics:** Often sudden onset, severe, described as "tearing," "ripping" or "sharp," located in the chest, back or between the shoulder blades, sometimes migrating as the dissection extends. [\(PubMed](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6910182/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) Central)

**Associated symptoms:** Syncope, acute neurologic deficits (stroke‐like), limb ischemia, visceral/renal ischemia, acute heart failure (if aortic regurgitation or tamponade), new murmur of aortic regurgitation. [\(NCBI](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK441963/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
